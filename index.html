The 7.9-turn gap is the entire investment thesis
FIH Group is a forgotten conglomerate — listed on AIM, trading at 268p, capitalised at £33.5m. It owns three things: a 175-year-old monopoly on the Falkland Islands, an institutional art logistics business that stores works for the Tate and the Royal Collection, and a passenger ferry that just got sold at a premium multiple to a strategic buyer.
The problem? Two loss-making divisions — FBS Construction and Momart Exhibitions — poison the optic. They drag the blended multiple to 3.9×, masking monopoly assets that belong at 10–13×. Nobody on AIM can see through the noise. But Aurelius can.
In March 2026, the board sold PHFC (the Gosport Ferry) to Collins River / Uber Boat at 12.6× pre-IFRS 16 EBITDA — a closed transaction, not a projection. The £11.6m of proceeds now sit on the balance sheet. Post-disposal EV compresses to £15.4m. The remaining portfolio can be acquired at 4.7× rump EBITDA. That's the entry. That's everything.
